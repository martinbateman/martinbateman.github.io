    Introduction

    In this lab you are going to examine some encryption techniques using Cryptool

    Instructions
  1. Using Cryptool Online (here) click Ciphers then Caesar.
    2.  
  2. Read the Description on the Caesar Cipher so you understand how it is working.
    3.  
  3. Go to the Cipher tab you will be presented with an interface to encrypt and decrypt text using a Caesar cipher.
    4.  
  4. Click Rot-13 at the bottom of the page.
    5.  
  5. Then click parse alphabet and enable Lower case and disable Upper case.
    6.  
  6. Now type a message into the Plaintext dialog box.
    7.  
  7. Note the encryption process is shown in real time at the bottom of the page.
    8.  

  8. Post your cipher text to the Teams chat in the General channel. Take one from the Teams chat and attempt to decrypt it.

    In the Week 7 - Encryption channel on Teams post the decryption (along with the cipher text) to the text you are attempting to decrypt.

    9.  
  9. Repeat the process with Monoalphabetic Substitution
    10.  
  10. Repeat the process with Homophonic Cipher
    11.  
    Crytanalysis
  11. Read the section on Cryptanalysis. Working in small groups, if possible or on your own, attempt to break the text shown on this page using a frequency analysis.
    12.  
  12. Each group should encrypt two messages using
    • Monoalphabetic Substitution
    • Homophonic Cipher
    13.  
  13. Swap with other groups, via the week 7 channel in Teams, and attempt a cryptanalysis in order to decipher the message.
